Should First World Countries intervene with Third World Countries?

The definition of a first-world country is “a term developed during the Cold War in the 1950s, originally referred to a country that wasn’t
aligned with the United States and other western nations in opposition to what was then the Soviet Union and its allies. Since the
collapse of the Soviet Union in 1991, the term's meaning has largely evolved. And the definition of a third-world country is “a phrase
that has been used historically to describe a class of economically developing nations... Today the preferred terminology is a developing
nation, an underdeveloped country, or a low and middle-income country”.

Since the Cold War, there have been debates on whether or not First World Countries should provide insight or dictate the decisions of
third world countries. This includes interventions, war, and invasion. In our opinion, we think third-world countries should be left alone.
One such example of first-world country intervention is the Vietnam War. The US was afraid of the spread of communism in South
Vietnam and the rest of Asia. So they first sent money, supplies, and military advisers to South Vietnam. Then, President Johnson
decided to deploy troops in March 1965. With this came ten years of war against the North Vietnamese and a war that would
define a decade and cause major changes. It also cost the US 58,220 deaths, 521 Australian deaths, 37 New Zealander deaths,
5,099 South Korean deaths, 200,000-250,000 South Vietnamese deaths, 1.1 Million North Vietnamese deaths, and 2,000,000
million civilian deaths.

As stated before, we think Third World Countries should be left alone. We think of this because of the outcomes of The First World
Countries intervening, providing insight, or dictating. This could cause small and large-scale conflicts. Undeveloped countries could be taken advantage of, exploited, worsen
the situation or worsen the population and put it into darkness. When countries become or are Third World Countries it is because they have large
numbers of people in poverty, they’re economically poor, non-industrialized, have a poor health care syste, and a low Human Development Index.
